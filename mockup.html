The shift

For two decades, the most valuable companies on the internet have been databases with interfaces. The app was how you reached the data. The data was the moat.

That model is breaking — from multiple directions at once.

AI agents are becoming the primary interface. When a user asks an agent to act on their behalf, the agent doesn't open your app — it needs structured, open data it can read and transact with. If your data isn't accessible, you're invisible.

Regulators are demanding portability. Users are expecting ownership. And open protocols are making it possible to publish data in formats that anyone — any app, any agent, any competitor — can build on.

The companies that adapt will define the standards their industries run on. The ones that don't will find themselves intermediated by someone who did.

AT Protocol is the most credible infrastructure for this transition.

Why a studio

This transition requires a different kind of thinking than your team is staffed for. Not because they aren't capable — because the mental model is different. Centralized architecture and decentralized protocols are fundamentally different design problems.

We provide protocol fluency. We've internalized how identity, data, and distribution work in a federated system so your team doesn't have to start from zero.

And when the work involves defining open standards, neutrality matters. A schema designed by one platform will be resisted by its competitors. We sit outside that dynamic — which is exactly where this work needs to happen.
